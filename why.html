TRIAL DESIGN

Prospective, RCT, 3 centers in Australia, randomized to ablation vs medical rate control.

WHO ARE THE PATIENTS?

NYHA+, idiopathic cardiomyopathy, LVEF≤45%.

Characters: N=68, Age 60, LVEF 33%, 25% longstanding persistent AF

WHAT DID THEY DO?

Rate Control: Goal = AFFIRM; mean HR=86.

Ablation: PVI + Box Lesion

ENDPOINT: Changes in LVEF in 6 months.

RESULTS

At 6 months, ablation SIGNIFICANTLY ↑LVEF by 18% (vs 4%); 58% had LVEF≥50%.

The Absence of LGE on MRI was associated with greater improvement in LVEF.

CABANA Trial

  • RCT, open-label, on catheter ablation for AF vs. drug therapy; N=2204.
  • No difference in composite primary endpoints of all-cause mortality, disabling stroke, serious bleeding, or cardiac arrest
  • Ablation reduced AF burden. Ablation was relatively safe.
  • 27.5% crossed over from drug to ablation. On-treatment analysis favored ablation.
  • Age<65 and HF showed significant benefits from ablation.

Packer et al. CABANA Trials. HRS 2018.
